Eddy Currents: Explained Visually

What Are Eddy Currents?

Eddy currents are circular electric currents induced within conductors by a changing magnetic field. They oppose the motion of the source and can produce heat or braking force.

The Physics Behind It

According to Faraday’s Law, a changing magnetic field induces an electromotive force:

$$ \mathcal{E} = -\frac{d\Phi_B}{dt} $$

And per Lenz’s Law, the induced current opposes the magnetic change that created it.

Real-World Applications

  • Non-destructive testing: Used to detect flaws in metal structures without damaging them.
  • Magnetic braking: Found in roller coasters, high-speed trains, and some gym equipment.
  • Induction heating: Used in metalworking and induction stoves.
